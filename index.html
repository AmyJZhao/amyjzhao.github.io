Stable Matching Problem

Implemented stable matching algorithm using Python that takes the same number of men and women and pairs them off based on preference in such a way that everybody ends up married to somebody and nobody is married to more than one person.

Parameters:
- User inputs a number N of how many men and women are to be paired off (ex: user input of 1 means 1 pair to be matched)
- Men are numbered 1 to N, and women are numbered 1 to N
- Each man ranks all the women in order from highest preference to lowest preference (ex: Man 1's preference list is 2 4 3 1, so he most prefers to propose to Woman 2 while Woman 1 is last)
- Each woman does the same and ranks all the men

Output:
A set of marriages with no instabilities (no man and woman in different pairs prefer each other to their current partners)

Data Structures:
- Two two-dimensional arrays, ManPref and WomanPref, for women's preference lists and men's preference lists
 - ManPref[m, i] denotes the ith woman on man m's preference list
 - WomanPref[w, i] denotes the ith man on woman w's preference list
- Linked list to hold set of free men who are not engaged
- Array Next that indicates for each man m the position of the next woman he will propose to on his preference list
 - Next[m] is initialized with 1 for all men m
- Array Current to store current engagements
 - Current[w] is initialized to None for all women to indicate they are not currently engaged
 - Current[w] is woman w's current partner m'
- N x N array Ranking where Ranking[w, m] contains the rank of man m in the sorted order of w's preferences (enables us to decide if man proposing to woman w is higher or lower on her preference list than her current fiance if she has one)
